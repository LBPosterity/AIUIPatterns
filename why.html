Why define these patterns?

Design patterns are solutions that are commonly repeated. Iterated upon inside intefaces over decades, to find what works.

The successful ones make up what we call best-practice.

For example:

What do you expect to happen when you click a dropdown?

This

OR

This

Obviously, the second one.

Over time & across many prodcuts, various patterns were tried for selecting options from a list. This is what has become ubiquitouse & familiar.

*Of course native browser controls influence this heavily but you take my point.

Enter AI...

Bringing a new paradigm in user experience design.

Moving user inputs from

Command based

Configure controls & options.

To

Intent based

I would like this outcome.

Previously...

Software used to be based on known inputs & outputs.

IF > THEN > IF > THEN > DONE

But...

AI is a powerfu probablistic natural language processor,

However, the output is not... predictable.

While generated results are getting better & better, they could range between:

100% Correct --- Partially Right --- Completely Fabricated

This new experience places the onus users to critically review their generated results.

The question then becomes...

What existing UX patterns are applicable & what new ones will we need to deliver effective AI experiences?

~ AI UX Patterns ~

Is an attempt to catalog & explore these patterns in an abstracted format. To serve as a useful guide as our AI adoption grows & matures.
